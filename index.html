Target Audience

 This  audience belongs to the peripheral processing group and would buy Peloton’s products with less consideration. Their lifestyle revolves around having fun, traveling, and interacting with people. They enjoy working out, but they do not consider themselves athletes. This group is composed of younger men and women in their 20s and 30s, primarily single. They are modern, follow fashion and celebrities, and have fewer responsibilities but still value being part of a community. This group of people are in the moderate to lower-income bracket and could live anywhere in the country.

 

 

 

 

Persuasion Theory  

Framing, priming, and the theory of reasoned action are all theories utilized in this website to apply a peripheral persuasion strategy. The framing and priming used can cause the audience to focus on the fun and entertaining part of Peloton and not on the bike's price, leading the target audience to purchase the products. For this reason, the cost of the bike is not flashed as much as the other website. Subjective norms from the Theory of Reasoned Action can also be addressed with references to social media where the audience can find likes or retweets to boost positive reaffirmations of the Peloton products (Fishbein 2008). 

 

 

 

  The Design of this page  

 For the peripheral approach, the website is strategically more lively, interactive, and fun. The design provides a clear contrast in color with the recognizable red P knob and black and white background. Nothing was placed arbitrarily to ensure visual connection, proximity, and alignment. The color scheme is also more vibrant to appeal to the younger, more carefree group.
